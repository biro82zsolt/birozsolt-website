About Me

I support my clients through a variety of methods and approaches in their personal development, overcoming everyday challenges, and achieving fulfillment in sports. My professional work is based on building authentic, trust-based relationships and maintaining high professional standards.

I obtained my first degree at the Kölcsey Ferenc Reformed Teacher Training College in 2007. During this time, I was already pursuing psychology studies at the University of Debrecen, where I graduated in 2010 as a psychologist and an English translator specialized in psychology. In 2016, I earned a degree in counseling psychology with a specialization in career and vocational guidance at Eötvös Loránd University (ELTE). In 2019, I completed the sports psychology training program at Semmelweis University. Among other method-specific trainings, I completed the Autogenic Training Therapist program of the Hungarian Association for Relaxation and Symbol Therapy in 2016. I am currently in training to become a qualified couples and family therapist.
